# Bangkok Travel Guide — Contoso Travel

## Overview
Bangkok is sensory overload in the best possible way — golden temples, chaotic street markets, rooftop bars with skyline views, and some of the best street food on the planet. It's affordable, welcoming, and endlessly surprising.

## Neighborhoods

### Rattanakosin (Old City)
The historic heart with the Grand Palace, Wat Pho (reclining Buddha), and Wat Arun across the river. Touristy but absolutely essential for first-timers. Visit temples in the morning before the heat peaks.

**Best for:** Temples, history, first-time visitors
**Getting there:** Chao Phraya Express Boat to Tha Tien pier, or taxi

### Khao San Road Area
The legendary backpacker strip. Love it or hate it, it's an experience — cheap hostels, market stalls, pad thai carts, and bucket cocktails. The surrounding streets (especially Soi Rambuttri) are calmer and more charming.

**Best for:** Budget travelers, nightlife, meeting other travelers
**Getting there:** Taxi or tuk-tuk (no direct BTS/MRT connection)

### Silom & Sathorn
Bangkok's business district by day, nightlife hub by night. Patpong Night Market is here, but the real gems are the rooftop bars — Sky Bar at Lebua (from "The Hangover Part II") and Vertigo at Banyan Tree.

**Best for:** Rooftop bars, upscale dining, business travelers
**Getting there:** BTS Silom Line to Sala Daeng or Chong Nonsi

### Sukhumvit
A long avenue with distinct sub-neighborhoods. Lower Sukhumvit (Nana, Asok, Phrom Phong) has international restaurants, shopping malls, and vibrant nightlife. Higher numbers get more local and residential.

**Best for:** Shopping, international dining, varied nightlife
**Getting there:** BTS Sukhumvit Line (multiple stations)

### Chinatown (Yaowarat)
Come here hungry. Yaowarat Road at night is Bangkok's best street food destination — seafood stalls, noodle shops, and dessert carts stretching for blocks. The daytime gold shops and temples are worth exploring too.

**Best for:** Street food, authentic atmosphere, photography
**Getting there:** MRT Blue Line to Wat Mangkon

## Food Guide

### Street Food
- **Yaowarat Road (Chinatown):** The undisputed champion. Must-try: grilled seafood at T&K Seafood (look for the green shirts), mango sticky rice from any cart. ~฿100-300/person
- **Soi Rambuttri:** Better pad thai than Khao San Road, at half the price. ~฿50-80/dish
- **Victory Monument:** Locals' spot for boat noodles — tiny bowls (฿15-20 each), you order 3-5 to make a meal. An experience.

### Restaurants
- **Raan Jay Fai:** Michelin-starred street food. The crab omelet is legendary. LONG wait — arrive 2pm for dinner service. ~฿1,000-2,000/person
- **Supanniga Eating Room (Thonglor):** Elevated Thai-Isan cuisine in a beautiful space. ~฿500-800/person
- **Som Tam Nua (Siam Square):** Best som tam (papaya salad) and fried chicken in the city. Always a queue at lunch. ~฿200-300/person

### Food Markets
- **Or Tor Kor Market:** Bangkok's most prestigious fresh market. Incredible tropical fruits, prepared curries, and the cleanest market you'll see. Try the durian if you're brave.
- **Chatuchak Weekend Market:** 15,000+ stalls. Overwhelming but incredible. Go early, bring water, wear comfortable shoes. Food stalls scattered throughout.

## Practical Tips

### Transport
- **BTS (Skytrain) and MRT (Subway)** are the fastest way around. Get a Rabbit card for BTS. ~฿20-60 per ride.
- **Grab (Southeast Asian Uber)** is reliable and affordable. Better than taxis for avoiding scams.
- **Tuk-tuks** are fun but ALWAYS negotiate the price before getting in. A reasonable ride is ฿100-150 for short distances. If they say "free" or "20 baht" — it's a scam to take you to a gem shop.
- **Chao Phraya Express Boat** is great for riverside attractions. ฿15-30 per ride.
- **Avoid taxis that won't use the meter.** Walk away and find another.

### Money
- **Thailand is very affordable.** A good meal is $3-8. A beer is $2-3.
- **ATMs charge ฿220 (~$6) foreign fee.** Withdraw large amounts to minimize fees. Or use Wise/Revolut cards.
- **Bargaining** is expected at markets (not in malls or restaurants). Start at 50-60% of the asking price.

### Weather
- **Hot season (March-May):** 35-40°C. Brutal. Stay hydrated, use 7-Eleven for AC breaks.
- **Rainy season (June-October):** Afternoon downpours (usually 1-2 hours). Carry a small umbrella.
- **Cool season (November-February):** 25-32°C. The "best" time — still warm but pleasant.

### Etiquette
- **Show respect for the monarchy** — it's the law, taken very seriously
- **Remove shoes** before entering temples and homes
- **Dress modestly at temples** — knees and shoulders covered (sarongs available to borrow)
- **Don't touch anyone's head** — it's considered the most sacred part of the body
- **Wai greeting** (palms pressed together with a slight bow) — return it when offered
