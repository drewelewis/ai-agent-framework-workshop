# Rome Travel Guide — Contoso Travel

## Overview
Rome is an open-air museum where you stumble upon 2,000-year-old ruins between your morning espresso and afternoon gelato. It's chaotic, romantic, and endlessly layered — every piazza has a story, every corner has a church worth visiting.

## Neighborhoods

### Centro Storico (Historic Center)
The Pantheon, Piazza Navona, Trevi Fountain, and the Spanish Steps are all here within walking distance. It's the most touristy area but undeniably spectacular. Wander the side streets for quieter discoveries.

**Best for:** First-time visitors, sightseeing, people-watching
**Getting there:** Metro A to Spagna or Barberini, then walk

### Trastevere
The bohemian heart of Rome. Cobblestone streets, ivy-covered buildings, and the city's best nightlife and casual dining. Cross the bridge from the Centro Storico and you'll immediately feel the difference — more local, more relaxed.

**Best for:** Nightlife, authentic dining, romantic walks
**Getting there:** Tram 8 from Largo Argentina, or walk across Ponte Sisto

### Testaccio
A real Roman working-class neighborhood that's become the city's food capital. Home to the old slaughterhouse (now a museum and event space), the Testaccio Market, and some of the best traditional Roman trattorias.

**Best for:** Food lovers, local atmosphere, off-the-beaten-path
**Getting there:** Metro B to Piramide

### Monti
Rome's oldest neighborhood, now its trendiest. Vintage shops, craft cocktail bars, independent bookshops, and a mix of tourists and locals. Via del Boschetto and Via dei Serpenti are the main streets.

**Best for:** Shopping, cocktails, trendy crowd, neighborhood vibe
**Getting there:** Metro B to Cavour

### Vatican Area (Prati)
Beyond the Vatican Museums and St. Peter's, the Prati neighborhood is a pleasant residential area with elegant shopping streets and some of Rome's best gelato. Less chaotic than the Centro Storico.

**Best for:** Vatican visit, quieter base, good gelato
**Getting there:** Metro A to Ottaviano

## Food Guide

### Traditional Roman Pasta (The Big Four)
Every Roman trattoria should master these:
- **Cacio e pepe:** Pecorino cheese and black pepper. Simple perfection. Best at: Da Felice (Testaccio), Roscioli (Centro)
- **Carbonara:** Egg, guanciale (pork cheek), pecorino. NO cream (ever). Best at: Da Danilo (Esquilino), Roscioli
- **Amatriciana:** Tomato, guanciale, pecorino. Best at: Bucatini (Trastevere), Da Felice
- **Gricia:** Guanciale and pecorino — carbonara without the egg. The Roman insider's choice.

### Pizza
- **Pizzarium (Prati):** Gabriele Bonci's legendary pizza al taglio (by the slice). Creative toppings on perfect crust. Always a line. ~€5-8 for a filling amount.
- **Da Remo (Testaccio):** Classic thin Roman pizza. Cash only, no frills, fantastic. ~€8-12 for a pizza + beer.
- **Supplì at any pizza shop:** Fried rice balls with mozzarella. The Roman answer to Sicilian arancini. ~€2 each.

### Gelato
- **Fatamorgana (multiple locations):** Artisanal, creative flavors. No artificial colors.
- **Giolitti (Centro):** Historic (since 1900) and still excellent. Skip the tourist gelato near the Trevi Fountain.
- **Rule:** If the gelato is piled high in bright neon colors, walk away. Real gelato is stored in covered metal containers.

### Coffee Culture
- **Espresso at the bar:** Stand at the counter, order "un caffè" (espresso), drink it in 2 minutes, pay ~€1.20. This is the Roman way.
- **Sitting costs more:** The same espresso at a table can cost €3-5 in touristy areas. The locals stand.
- **Don't order cappuccino after 11am** — Italians consider it a breakfast drink only.
- **Sant'Eustachio Il Caffè:** Near the Pantheon. Allegedly Rome's best espresso.

## Practical Tips

### Transport
- **Walk everything in the center.** Rome's best experiences are between the sights, not at them.
- **Metro is limited** — only 3 lines. Useful for Colosseum (Metro B, Colosseo) and Vatican (Metro A, Ottaviano).
- **Buses are unreliable** but cover more ground. Buy tickets at tabacchi shops BEFORE boarding. €1.50 for 100 minutes.
- **Taxis:** Official taxis are WHITE with a meter. Fixed rate from airports: €50 from Fiumicino, €31 from Ciampino.
- **Cobblestones everywhere** — wear comfortable, flat shoes. Seriously.

### Money
- **Cards work in most restaurants** but carry €20-50 for small shops, market stalls, and coffee bars.
- **Coperto (cover charge):** €2-4 per person added to restaurant bills. This is normal, not a scam.
- **Tipping:** Not expected. Rounding up or leaving €1-2 is generous.

### Safety
- **Pickpockets** target tourists at the Colosseum, Trevi Fountain, and on bus line 64 (Vatican route). Be alert.
- **Gladiators at the Colosseum** who want photos will demand €20+. Avoid them.
- **Rose sellers and bracelet scammers** — firmly say "No, grazie" and keep walking.

### Etiquette
- **Dress code for churches** — shoulders and knees must be covered. St. Peter's enforces this strictly.
- **Dinner starts at 8pm** at the earliest. Many restaurants don't open until 7:30pm.
- **Sunday lunch** is sacred family time. Restaurants may be packed or closed.
