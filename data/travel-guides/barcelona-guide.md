# Barcelona Travel Guide — Contoso Travel

## Overview
Barcelona is where Gothic architecture meets Gaudí's surrealism, where you eat dinner at 10pm, and where the beach is a 15-minute metro ride from mountain trails. It's Spain's most cosmopolitan city with a fierce Catalan identity.

## Neighborhoods

### Gothic Quarter (Barri Gòtic)
The medieval heart of Barcelona. Narrow stone alleys open into hidden plazas, with the stunning Barcelona Cathedral as the centerpiece. Best explored by getting deliberately lost. Watch for pickpockets in crowded areas.

**Best for:** History, atmosphere, evening walks
**Getting there:** Metro L3 (Green) to Jaume I or L4 (Yellow) to Jaume I

### El Born
Adjacent to the Gothic Quarter but trendier. Home to the Picasso Museum, Santa Maria del Mar basilica, and some of Barcelona's best cocktail bars and boutiques. The Passeig del Born boulevard comes alive at night.

**Best for:** Art, nightlife, boutique shopping, cocktails
**Getting there:** Metro L4 to Jaume I or Barceloneta

### La Barceloneta
Barcelona's beach neighborhood. A former fishing village with narrow streets, seafood restaurants, and a long sandy beach. Touristy along the waterfront, authentic a few streets back.

**Best for:** Beach days, seafood, casual atmosphere
**Getting there:** Metro L4 to Barceloneta

### Eixample
The grid-planned neighborhood that's home to most of Gaudí's masterpieces — Casa Batlló, Casa Milà (La Pedrera), and the Sagrada Família. Wide boulevards, elegant buildings, and excellent dining.

**Best for:** Architecture, upscale dining, Gaudí fans
**Getting there:** Metro L2/L3/L4 to Passeig de Gràcia

### Gràcia
A former independent village that maintains its bohemian, local character. Plazas filled with outdoor cafes, independent shops, vintage stores, and a young creative crowd. Park Güell is at the top of the neighborhood.

**Best for:** Local vibe, independent shops, Park Güell
**Getting there:** Metro L3 to Fontana

## Food Guide

### Tapas
- **Bar Cañete (El Raval):** Upscale tapas at the bar. The grilled razor clams and croquetas are legendary. Expect to spend €40-50/person. No reservations — queue early.
- **El Xampanyet (El Born):** Tiny, tiled, atmospheric. House cava by the glass and simple but perfect tapas. Cash only. ~€15-20/person
- **La Pepita (Gràcia):** Creative tapas in a relaxed neighborhood setting. Try the "bikini" truffle sandwich. ~€20-30/person

### Seafood
- **La Mar Salada (Barceloneta):** Local favorite for paella and arroz negro (black rice). Much better value than the beachfront tourist traps. ~€20-30/person
- **Can Paixano (La Barceloneta):** Standing-room-only cava bar with incredible sandwiches and seafood. Chaotic, loud, authentic. ~€10-15/person

### Markets
- **La Boqueria (Las Ramblas):** Iconic but very touristy. Best early morning before 10am. Get a fresh fruit smoothie and jamón ibérico at the back stalls.
- **Mercat de Santa Caterina (El Born):** Locals' market with a stunning wavy roof. Better value and fewer crowds than La Boqueria. Great for lunch.

### Don't Miss
- **Pan con tomate:** Bread rubbed with tomato, olive oil, and salt. Simple perfection.
- **Vermouth hour:** Sunday noon vermouth at any local bar is a Barcelona tradition.
- **Dinner time:** Restaurants don't fill up until 9-10pm. Eating at 7pm = dining alone.

## Practical Tips

### Transport
- **T-Casual card** (10 metro/bus rides for ~€11.35) is the best value. Buy at any metro station.
- **Walking** is the best way to explore Gothic Quarter and El Born — the metro skips the atmosphere.
- **Airport to city:** Aerobus (€7.75, 35 min to Plaça Catalunya) is the easiest option.

### Money
- **Cards accepted almost everywhere** — but carry €20-30 for small tapas bars and markets
- **Tipping:** Not expected but 5-10% for good restaurant service is appreciated
- **Menu del día:** Lunch set menus (€12-18) are the best deal in restaurants. Usually 3 courses + drink.

### Safety
- **Pickpockets** are the #1 concern, especially on Las Ramblas, in the metro, and at La Boqueria. Use a front pocket or cross-body bag.
- **The city is very safe** otherwise — walking at night is fine in most neighborhoods.
- **Beach safety:** Don't leave valuables unattended. Use locker services.

### Etiquette
- **Learn some Catalan** — "Bon dia" (good morning) and "Gràcies" (thank you) are appreciated
- **Siesta is real** — many small shops close 2-4pm
- **Sunday closures** — most shops closed on Sundays except in tourist areas
